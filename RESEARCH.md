I Used To Make Art For Humans: An Experiment in Agentic Art Selection
RESEARCH DOCUMENT

Experiment by Transient Labs
Agent artworks by 5TR4N0
6/8/2026

ABSTRACT

It is predicted that by 2030, 50% of commerce done online will be facilitated by artificial intelligence (AI) agents, rather than humans. Can an agent effectively purchase art that pleases its human counterpart, and if so, what does this mean for artists working today when the audience they need to please becomes AI, rather than purely human? The following experiment is designed to answer these questions and test their results, attempting to predict how art collecting and practice would function in an agentic future.

CHAPTERS
1. Tuning the Agents
Using existing popular AI models (Claude, ChatGPT, and Google's Gemini), we will test aesthetic preferences and effectiveness when evaluating physical and digital artworks.
Objective
Evaluate leading AI models and determine whether they can produce stable, defensible, and differentiated aesthetic judgments when reviewing artwork.
Evaluation Criteria
Consistency: The same artwork reviewed multiple times by the same configured model should produce similar conclusions and ratings.
Taste Differentiation: Distinct agent profiles should produce meaningfully different evaluations of the same artwork.
Critique Quality: Reviews should demonstrate interpretation, evaluation, and preference rather than simple description.
Distribution of Outcomes: Agents should neither approve nor reject nearly everything.

Success State
Review outcomes remain substantially consistent across repeated evaluations of the same artwork.
Distinct agent profiles produce meaningfully different judgments.
Reviews demonstrate interpretation rather than simple image description.
Approval and rejection rates remain reasonably balanced across a large sample set.
Proceed Condition
Proceed to Chapter 2 once a set of baseline agent configurations has demonstrated stable judgment, differentiated taste, balanced decision making, and useful written critiques.
2. Establishing a Review
After tuning the models, they will review images of 20 public-domain paintings with the goal of establishing an effective review format and evaluation criteria.
Objective
Develop a review format that allows agents to evaluate artworks consistently while producing outputs that are interpretable by human observers.
Evaluation Criteria
Clarity: Reviews should be understandable by someone without knowledge of the underlying model.
Repeatability: The same artwork reviewed under the same conditions should produce a similar review structure.
Comparative Usefulness: Reviews should allow meaningful comparison between artworks.
Coverage: Reviews should evaluate multiple dimensions of an artwork.
Signal Strength: Reviews should explain outcomes without overwhelming users with reasoning data.
Success State
Reviews follow a consistent structure.
Different artworks produce meaningfully different evaluations.
Human observers can understand how decisions were reached.
Reviews support comparison across large sets of artwork.
Review outputs can be efficiently processed at scale.
Human Plausibility Gate
Before advancing, reviews should be presented to experienced artists, collectors, and curators. The objective is not agreement, but whether the critique feels like a legitimate opinion from an observer with coherent taste.
Proceed Condition
Proceed to Chapter 3 once a review framework has been established that is understandable, repeatable, scalable, and useful for comparing artworks.
3. Opening the System
With the agents and review prepared, approximately 1,000 agents will be released to the public, embodied in digital artworks. Owners will be able to tune their agent to their own preferences and follow its reviews and decisions.
4. Opening the Input
A submission page will allow artists to submit work for evaluation. Decisions and review logs will be published live. Additional artworks may be sourced from Transient.xyz, public-domain collections, or other repositories if required.
5. Live Feedback Loop
A physical studio will be established with live camera input and agent feedback displays. Artists will create work live and gather agent evaluations whenever they deem a work complete. This portion of the experiment will be livestreamed.
6. Ongoing Exposure
The agents will continue to function and evolve, tuned to the tastes and preferences of their holders.

At any step, certain aspects of the experiment may change if there is a path which more effectively tests the central question. This is only a preliminary outline.
